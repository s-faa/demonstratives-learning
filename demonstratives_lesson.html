What Are Demonstratives?

Demonstratives are words that point to specific things, people, or places. They help us identify which one we are talking about.

Demonstrative Pronouns

These pronouns replace nouns in a sentence:

  • This - Refers to something close (e.g., "This is my book.")
  • That - Refers to something far (e.g., "That is your bag.")
  • These - Refers to plural things close (e.g., "These are my keys.")
  • Those - Refers to plural things far (e.g., "Those are your shoes.")

Example: This is a cat. That is a dog.

Demonstrative Adjectives

These adjectives describe nouns and show their position:

  • This - Close singular noun (e.g., "This book is interesting.")
  • That - Far singular noun (e.g., "That chair is red.")
  • These - Close plural noun (e.g., "These apples are sweet.")
  • Those - Far plural noun (e.g., "Those stars are bright.")

Example: I like this song. I don’t like that noise.

Tips for Understanding

For ADHD learners: Break the topic into small chunks. Practice one demonstrative at a time (e.g., today focus on this).

For vision challenges: Use high-contrast visuals or tactile objects to practice (e.g., "Touch this cube, then that sphere.").

Practice Exercise

Fill in the blanks with the correct demonstrative pronoun or adjective:

  1. ________ is a beautiful flower. (Close)
  2. I don’t like ________ colors. (Far, plural)
  3. Can you hand me ________ pen? (Close)
  4. ________ birds are flying away. (Far, plural)
